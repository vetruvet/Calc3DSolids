General Help   

Welcome the the Calculus 3D Solids Program!

These help files will (hopefully) help you understand how to use this program, and clear up any questions about its operation

This program is designed to demonstrate how solids of revolution and solids with known cross-sections are formed. With a 2D chalkboard, it is oftentimes hard to understand and demonstrate the concept of solids in Calculus. That's where this program comes in.

NOTE: This program is NOT a calculator!!! Don't expect it to give you the volume or area or whatever.


The basic usage of this program is:

  1. Enter the equations. For tips on writing equations, please consult the Equations section.

For Solids of Revolution:

  1. Enter the axis, including whether it's horizontal or vertical. Constants like PI and E can be used here.
  2. Select the method of approximation, either discs/washers or shells.
  3. Enter the number of discs/washers or shells to be shown.

For Solids with Known Cross-Sections:

  1. Select the shape for the cross-section
  2. For shapes with a constant height, enter the height value. Constants like PI and E can be used here.
  3. Select which axis the cross-sections are perpendicular to and how many cross-section cuts there should be.
  1. Click the corresponding graph button to view a 2D or 3D graph for the given input. For tips of viewing the graphs, please consult the Graphing section
  2. Examine the graphs and experiment with different viewing angles
